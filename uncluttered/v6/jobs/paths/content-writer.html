Content Writer / Copywriter Path

Words that convert. The tools and workflow that get writers hired by top companies.

Your Path to Hired

1
Writing Tool

Google Docs or Notion

Free

Clean, collaborative, universal. Every content team uses one of these. Master the commenting and suggestion features, you'll use them daily.

2
Grammar & Style

Grammarly + Hemingway App

Free

Grammarly catches errors. Hemingway forces clarity. Use both on everything you write. Clean copy is the baseline, not the goal.

3
SEO Writing

Ahrefs Webmaster + Search Console

Free

Content that ranks > content that doesn't. Learn keyword research basics. Writers who understand SEO earn significantly more.

4
AI Assistant

Claude

Free tier

Brainstorm angles, draft outlines, research topics. AI is your co-pilot, not your replacement. The best writers use AI to think faster, not write for them.

5
Portfolio

Notion or Framer

Free tiers

Curate your 5-10 best pieces. Organize by type: blog posts, landing pages, emails, social. Make it dead simple for editors to see your range.

6
Start Publishing

Medium + LinkedIn + Substack

Free

Publish regularly. Build a body of work people can find. Consistency beats perfection, one post per week builds a serious portfolio in months.

7
Pitching

Hunter.io + LinkedIn

Free tier

Find editors' emails. Send concise pitches. Get published in real publications. One byline in a known publication opens dozens of doors.

What Hiring Managers Actually Look For

📰

Published clips are everything

Editors and hiring managers want to see your work live, in real publications. Start a blog or newsletter TODAY. Even self-published work counts if it's good.

🔍

Learn SEO, seriously

Writers who drive organic traffic get paid more. Period. Understanding keyword research and on-page SEO makes you 2x more valuable than a pure creative writer.

🎯

Develop a specialty

Generalist writers are replaceable. The "SaaS content writer" or "fintech copywriter" gets hired faster and paid more. Pick a niche and own it.

✉️

Start a newsletter TODAY

A newsletter proves you can write consistently, build an audience, and understand metrics. "500 subscribers" on your resume is instant credibility.

💰
