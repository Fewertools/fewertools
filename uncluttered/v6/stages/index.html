Know when to level up

Stages aren't permanent. Here's how to recognize when you're ready to move on.

ðŸ’¡

Starting Exploration

You have ideas to validate, problems to understand, and assumptions to test.

Move to Building when:
  • You've talked to 10+ potential users
  • You can describe the MVP in one sentence
  • You've validated people will pay (or at least sign up)
  • You're itching to code, not research

ðŸ”¨

Building Shipping

You're turning ideas into reality. Code is being written, features are shipping.

Move to Growing when:
  • You have real users (not just friends)
  • People are paying or showing strong intent
  • You need to understand user behavior
  • Support requests are becoming a thing

Growing Scaling

You have traction. Now it's about understanding, optimizing, and scaling.

Consider Simplifying when:
  • Your tool stack has grown organically (chaos)
  • You're paying for overlapping features
  • Onboarding new team members is painful
  • You spend more time in tools than on work

Simplifying Clarity

Step back, audit everything, cut ruthlessly, and regain focus.

You've succeeded when:
  • Every tool has a clear, singular purpose
  • New team members can onboard in a day
  • Your monthly SaaS bill makes sense
  • You feel calm, not overwhelmed
