Why I Built This

Every "best tools" list I found had 50+ options. Every comparison led to another comparison. I'd spend hours researching the "perfect" tool instead of actually building.

Sound familiar?

I realized that tool research had become a form of productive procrastination. The more options I explored, the less I shipped.

So I built Fewer Tools: the resource I wished existed. One opinionated recommendation per category. Clear guidance based on your stage. No endless comparisons or affiliate-driven listicles.

The goal isn't to find the "perfect" tool. It's to find a good-enough tool and get back to building.
