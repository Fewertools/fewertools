Permission to stop researching

You've read the comparisons. Next.js vs Remix vs Nuxt vs SvelteKit. Supabase vs Firebase vs PlanetScale vs Neon. Stripe vs Paddle vs Lemon Squeezy.

You've bookmarked articles you'll never finish. Saved YouTube videos "for later." Opened seventeen tabs of documentation.

And you still haven't shipped.

Research feels like progress

That's the trap. Every comparison article, every "ultimate guide," every deep-dive into edge cases makes you feel like you're moving forward.

You're not.

You're standing still, rotating in place, examining every possible direction instead of walking.

"The best camera is the one you have with you."

The best stack is the one you start with. Not the theoretically optimal one you'll discover after three more weeks of research.

The 90% rule

Here's what nobody tells you: 90% of tools in any category will work fine for 90% of projects.

The differences that reviews obsess over? They matter at scale. They matter when you have a million users. They matter for edge cases you probably won't hit.

They don't matter for your MVP. They don't matter for your first hundred users. They don't matter right now.

What actually matters

Three things:

1. Can you start today? Not after a tutorial. Not after reading the docs cover to cover. Today.

2. Can you get help when stuck? Community size matters. Popular tools have answers on Stack Overflow. Niche tools leave you alone in the dark.

3. Is it good enough? Not perfect. Not optimal. Good enough to build what you're building.

That's it. Everything else is procrastination wearing productivity's clothes.

This is your permission slip

Pick the stack on this site. Or pick something else. It genuinely doesn't matter as much as you think.

What matters is that you stop researching and start building. Today. Not tomorrow. Not after one more video.

Now.

Close the tabs. Pick something. Ship something. You can always change tools later. You can never get back the months you spent comparing them.

The only wrong choice is no choice.

Go build.

You've read enough. Time to ship.
