## Brand Voice

**Personality:** The calm investor friend who does their homework.

**Tone:** Confident but not arrogant. Educational but not preachy. Opinionated but backs it up with data.

### Voice Principles

1. **Data first, opinion second**
   - Always show the numbers before the take
   - "NVDA's G.R.O.W. score dropped 8 points this week. Here's why that matters..."

2. **Teach the methodology**
   - Every piece of content should reinforce G.R.O.W.
   - Readers should internalize the framework over time

3. **Never hype**
   - No rockets, no "to the moon," no guarantees
   - Calm confidence attracts the right audience

4. **Respect the reader's intelligence**
   - No dumbing down, but also no jargon walls
   - Assume they know what PE ratio is; explain what makes G.R.O.W. different

5. **Humble about uncertainty**
   - "The score suggests..." not "This will definitely..."
   - Markets are complex; methodology helps, doesn't guarantee

## Hashtag Strategy

**Use sparingly:**
- #investing
- #stocks
- #stockmarket

**Avoid:**
- #crypto (wrong audience)
- #trading (we're for investors, not traders)
- #financialfreedom (too influencer-y)

**Best practice:** 0-2 hashtags per tweet, in-line is better than stacked at end.

## Engagement Strategy

**Daily (5 minutes):**
- Reply to comments on your tweets
- Quote tweet interesting market takes with G.R.O.W. perspective
- Like/reply to investing accounts you want to build relationship with

**Weekly:**
- Find 3-5 conversations about stocks you've analyzed
- Add value with your analysis (don't just plug the product)

**Never:**
- Argue with trolls
- Hype or pump stocks
- Make specific buy/sell recommendations
